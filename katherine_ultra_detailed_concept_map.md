### **Function**: Breaks down food into nutrients for absorption and elimination of waste

## **üü¢ LEVEL 1: MAJOR ORGANS OF DIGESTIVE TRACT (Question 1)**

### **1. ORAL CAVITY (Mouth)**
**Function**: Mechanical breakdown (chewing/mastication) + chemical digestion (salivary amylase)
**Components**: 
- Teeth: Mechanical breakdown
- Tongue: Mixing + taste
- Salivary glands: Produce saliva with amylase

### **2. SALIVARY GLANDS** 
**Function**: Produce saliva containing salivary amylase (breaks down carbohydrates)
**Types**: Parotid, submandibular, sublingual

### **3. ESOPHAGUS**
**Function**: Transport food from pharynx to stomach via peristalsis
**Key Feature**: Muscular tube with mucous lining

### **4. STOMACH**
**Function**: Mechanical churning + chemical digestion (HCl + pepsin for proteins)
**Components**: Cardia, fundus, body, pylorus

### **5. SMALL INTESTINE**
**Function**: Major site of chemical digestion and nutrient absorption
**Sections**: Duodenum, jejunum, ileum

### **6. LARGE INTESTINE (Colon)**
**Function**: Water absorption + formation of feces
**Sections**: Cecum, ascending colon, transverse colon, descending colon, sigmoid colon

### **7. RECTUM**
**Function**: Temporary storage of feces before elimination

### **8. ANUS**
**Function**: Elimination of feces through anal sphincters

## **üü£ LEVEL 2: ACCESSORY ORGANS (Question 2)**

### **1. LIVER**
**Function**: Produces bile for fat emulsification
**Enzymes/Chemicals**: Bile (contains bile salts)
**Breaks Down**: Fats (emulsification, not chemical breakdown)

### **2. GALLBLADDER**
**Function**: Stores and concentrates bile from liver
**Enzymes/Chemicals**: Bile (concentrated)
**Breaks Down**: Fats (when released into duodenum)

### **3. PANCREAS**
**Function**: Produces pancreatic juice with digestive enzymes
**Enzymes/Chemicals**: 
- Pancreatic amylase (carbohydrates ‚Üí sugars)
- Trypsin & chymotrypsin (proteins ‚Üí peptides/amino acids)
- Lipase (fats ‚Üí fatty acids + glycerol)
- Sodium bicarbonate (neutralizes stomach acid)

## **üîµ LEVEL 3: INGESTION PROCESS (Question 3)**

### **Organs Involved**:
1. **Oral Cavity**: Food enters, mechanical breakdown begins
2. **Teeth**: Chewing (mastication)
3. **Tongue**: Mixing food with saliva
4. **Salivary Glands**: Secrete saliva

### **Enzymes**:
- **Salivary Amylase**: Starts carbohydrate digestion (starch ‚Üí maltose)

### **Mechanical Breakdown**:
- Chewing (mastication)
- Tongue movement mixing

### **Chemical Breakdown**:
- Salivary amylase acting on carbohydrates

### **Process Flow**:
```
Food enters mouth ‚Üí Teeth chew ‚Üí Tongue mixes with saliva ‚Üí 
Salivary amylase breaks down carbs ‚Üí Bolus formed ‚Üí 
Swallowed through pharynx ‚Üí Enters esophagus
```

## **üü° LEVEL 4: DIGESTION PROCESS (Question 4)**

### **Two Types of Digestion**:

#### **A. MECHANICAL DIGESTION**
**Organs**: 
1. Mouth (chewing)
2. Stomach (churning)
3. Small intestine (segmentation)

**Purpose**: Physically break food into smaller pieces

#### **B. CHEMICAL DIGESTION**
**Organs + Enzymes**:
1. **Mouth**: Salivary amylase (carbs)
2. **Stomach**: 
   - HCl (activates pepsin, denatures proteins)
   - Pepsin (proteins ‚Üí peptides)
3. **Small Intestine**:
   - Pancreatic enzymes (amylase, trypsin, lipase)
   - Brush border enzymes (maltase, lactase, sucrase)
   - Bile (fat emulsification)

### **Process Flow**:
```
Stomach: HCl + Pepsin ‚Üí Proteins broken down
Duodenum: Bile + Pancreatic enzymes ‚Üí All macronutrients broken down
Jejunum: Brush border enzymes ‚Üí Final breakdown
```

## **üü† LEVEL 5: ABSORPTION PROCESS (Question 5)**

### **Primary Site**: SMALL INTESTINE

#### **A. Structural Adaptations for Absorption**:
1. **Villi**: Finger-like projections increasing surface area
2. **Microvilli**: Further increase surface area ("brush border")
3. **Lacteals**: Lymph vessels for fat absorption
4. **Capillaries**: Blood vessels for nutrient transport

#### **B. What Gets Absorbed Where**:
1. **Duodenum**: Iron, calcium, magnesium
2. **Jejunum**: Carbohydrates, proteins, fats, water-soluble vitamins
3. **Ileum**: Vitamin B‚ÇÅ‚ÇÇ, bile salts

#### **C. Absorption Mechanisms**:
1. **Simple Diffusion**: Water, lipids, some minerals
2. **Facilitated Diffusion**: Fructose
3. **Active Transport**: Glucose, galactose, amino acids
4. **Endocytosis**: Some large molecules

#### **D. Enzymes Involved**:
- **Brush border enzymes**: Complete final digestion before absorption
- **No mechanical breakdown** during absorption

## **üî¥ LEVEL 6: ELIMINATION/DEFECATION (Question 6)**

### **Organs Involved**:
1. **Large Intestine**: Water absorption, feces formation
2. **Rectum**: Feces storage
3. **Anus**: Elimination

### **Process Steps**:
1. **Water Absorption**: In colon (500ml ‚Üí 150ml feces)
2. **Feces Formation**: Undigested material + bacteria + water
3. **Storage**: Rectum holds feces
4. **Elimination**: Anal sphincters relax, feces expelled

### **No Enzymes Involved** in elimination
### **No Mechanical/Chemical Breakdown** in elimination

### **Anal Sphincters**:
1. **Internal**: Involuntary smooth muscle
2. **External**: Voluntary skeletal muscle

## **üü¢ LEVEL 7: MECHANICAL vs CHEMICAL BREAKDOWN (Question 7)**

### **MECHANICAL BREAKDOWN**
**Definition**: Physical breakdown of food into smaller pieces
**Purpose**: Increase surface area for chemical digestion
**Examples**:
- Chewing (mouth)
- Churning (stomach)
- Segmentation (small intestine)

**Organ Example**: Stomach
**Process**: Churning mixes food with gastric juices
**No enzymes involved** in mechanical breakdown

### **CHEMICAL BREAKDOWN**
**Definition**: Enzymatic breakdown of food molecules
**Purpose**: Break complex molecules into absorbable units
**Examples**:
- Salivary amylase on carbohydrates
- Pepsin on proteins
- Lipase on fats

**Organ Example**: Stomach
**Enzyme**: Pepsin
**Process**: Pepsin breaks proteins into peptides
**Substrate**: Proteins ‚Üí Products: Peptides

### **Comparison Table**:
| Aspect | Mechanical | Chemical |
|--------|------------|----------|
| **Type** | Physical | Enzymatic |
| **Purpose** | Increase surface area | Break molecular bonds |
| **Example Organ** | Mouth/Stomach | Stomach/Small Intestine |
| **Example Process** | Chewing | Pepsin action on proteins |
| **Energy Use** | Muscle energy | Enzyme catalysis |
| **End Result** | Smaller pieces | Simpler molecules |

## **üü£ LEVEL 8: STOMACH DETAILS (Question 8)**

### **A. Major Parts of Stomach**:
1. **Cardia**: Junction with esophagus
2. **Fundus**: Upper rounded portion
3. **Body**: Main central region
4. **Pylorus**: Lower portion connecting to duodenum

### **B. Enzymes Used**:
1. **Pepsin** (active form of pepsinogen)
   - **Function**: Breaks proteins into peptides
   - **Activation**: HCl converts pepsinogen ‚Üí pepsin
2. **Gastric Lipase** (minor role)
   - **Function**: Breaks down some fats

### **C. Mechanical Processes**:
1. **Churning**: Mixing food with gastric juices
2. **Peristalsis**: Wave-like contractions moving food toward pylorus

### **D. Chemical Processes**:
1. **HCl Secretion**:
   - Activates pepsin
   - Denatures proteins
   - Kills bacteria
2. **Pepsin Action**: Proteins ‚Üí Peptides
3. **Mucus Production**: Protects stomach lining

### **E. Process Flow in Stomach**:
```
Food enters ‚Üí Churning mixes with gastric juice ‚Üí 
HCl activates pepsinogen ‚Üí Pepsin breaks proteins ‚Üí 
Gastric lipase acts on some fats ‚Üí Chyme formed ‚Üí 
Released to duodenum via pyloric sphincter
```

## **üîµ LEVEL 9: SMALL INTESTINE DETAILS (Question 9)**

### **A. Major Parts of Small Intestine**:
1. **Duodenum** (25cm/10in)
   - Receives chyme from stomach
   - Receives bile and pancreatic juice
2. **Jejunum** (2.5m/8ft)
   - Major site of nutrient absorption
3. **Ileum** (3.6m/12ft)
   - Vitamin B‚ÇÅ‚ÇÇ and bile salt absorption
   - Connects to large intestine at ileocecal valve

### **B. Enzymes Used**:
#### **Pancreatic Enzymes (released into duodenum)**:
1. **Pancreatic Amylase**: Carbohydrates ‚Üí Disaccharides
2. **Trypsin & Chymotrypsin**: Proteins ‚Üí Peptides/Amino acids
3. **Pancreatic Lipase**: Fats ‚Üí Fatty acids + Glycerol
4. **Ribonuclease & Deoxyribonuclease**: Nucleic acids ‚Üí Nucleotides

#### **Brush Border Enzymes (on intestinal wall)**:
1. **Maltase**: Maltose ‚Üí Glucose
2. **Lactase**: Lactose ‚Üí Glucose + Galactose
3. **Sucrase**: Sucrose ‚Üí Glucose + Fructose
4. **Peptidases**: Peptides ‚Üí Amino acids

#### **Liver/Gallbladder Contribution**:
- **Bile**: Emulsifies fats (mechanical, not enzymatic)

### **C. Mechanical Processes**:
1. **Segmentation**: Mixing chyme with digestive juices
2. **Peristalsis**: Slow movement toward large intestine

### **D. Chemical Processes**:
1. **Neutralization**: Sodium bicarbonate neutralizes stomach acid
2. **Carbohydrate Digestion**: Amylase ‚Üí Disaccharides ‚Üí Monosaccharides
3. **Protein Digestion**: Trypsin ‚Üí Peptides ‚Üí Amino acids
4. **Fat Digestion**: Bile emulsification ‚Üí Lipase breakdown

### **E. Absorption Specializations**:
1. **Villi & Microvilli**: 600x surface area increase
2. **Lacteals**: Fat absorption into lymphatic system
3. **Capillaries**: Water-soluble nutrient absorption into blood

## **üü° LEVEL 10: LARGE INTESTINE DETAILS (Question 10)**

### **A. Major Parts of Large Intestine**:
1. **Cecum**: Pouch receiving ileum contents
2. **Appendix**: Attached to cecum (immune function)
3. **Colon**:
   - **Ascending Colon**: Up right side
   - **Transverse Colon**: Across abdomen
   - **Descending Colon**: Down left side
   - **Sigmoid Colon**: S-shaped, connects to rectum
4. **Rectum**: Storage of feces
5. **Anal Canal**: Elimination

### **B. Enzymes Used**:
**NO DIGESTIVE ENZYMES PRODUCED**
- Bacterial enzymes ferment some undigested material
- Bacteria produce Vitamin K and some B vitamins

### **C. Mechanical Processes**:
1. **Haustral Churning**: Slow mixing movements
2. **Mass Movements**: Strong peristalsis 3-4 times daily
3. **Defecation Reflex**: Coordinated elimination

### **D. Chemical Processes**:
1. **Water Absorption**: 500ml chyme ‚Üí 150ml feces
2. **Electrolyte Absorption**: Na‚Å∫, Cl‚Åª, some vitamins
3. **Bacterial Fermentation**: Undigested carbohydrates ‚Üí gases + acids
4. **Vitamin Synthesis**: Bacteria produce Vitamin K, biotin, B vitamins

### **E. Functions**:
1. **Water & Electrolyte Absorption**
2. **Feces Formation**
3. **Vitamin Production**
4. **Waste Storage**
5. **Elimination**

### **F. Feces Composition**:
- 75% water
- Undigested material
- Bacteria
- Bile pigments
- Electrolytes

Chemical: Enzymes throughout system
